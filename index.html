2025-09-21

Quantamaniac is something I wanted to work on for a long time. It's a video game that I want to build, but making a video game as a solo-dev is a monumental task. After several discussions with ChatGPT, I realised that the overall scope I have in my mind will take at least 3 years to complete. So I need to first start small, build an MVP and see if people even like the concept. Then I can plan on getting some funds, or to hire someone to share the workload.

This weekend I simply tried scoping out the project, going into details, making a clearer picture of the world in my mind. I do have a full-time job where I'll be occupied most of the weekdays, so taking out time on a weekday will be difficult. I do plan to take out 1 hr at least on the weekdays, and more time on the weekends. This is not a good start tbh, but It has been far too long that I have ignored working on any side project - I have to start somewhere, and that's what I'm doing.

I have decided to go with Godot Engine for developing the game, as the game I'm building is a 2D game, and it needs to be light weight. I am hoping I'll be able to get relevant tutorials and resources online and will be relying on ChatGPT a lot. Unity Engine was more familiar as I have already developed a game on that before during the lockdown period, but I don't trust the company anymore after the recent pricing model debacle. Also, Godot has grown a lot now, and has an active dev community around it who help each other.

The biggest challeng isn't development though, it's the content. I need to create a ton of assets, videos, dialogues, etc. I am hoping I'll be able to leverage GenAI for the content. Another big aspect will be handling multi-player. But I think I'll first make a single-player version and then see how I can move to multi-player. Another thing is that the game has to be available on all platforms - mainly mobile. But that should be fine, Godot can handle that.
